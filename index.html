A Poem for Mali

For I could travel the world
Through Mountain peak to Ocean depth
Through violent flames and freezing breaths
Where oceans roar and forests swept
your light would glimmer; my soul is kept

Her eyes, like the northern lights
illuminate the darkest skies
A glow that holds the endless nights
bringing the greatest of highs
Clinging on time

Her laugh, worth more than a dime
Alluring; a melody so sublime
soft and sweet yet strong and sure
running laps without a doubt
perhaps I'm faltering without a cure

Her hair, Rapunzel wouldn't compare
threads of beauty beyond all flair
A flowing cascade of curls so fair
like a mermaid swimming in the air

She walks in beauty, not by what she wears
but the beauty in her heart, so rare
A spirit that glows with kindness and care
thoughtful, funny with a mind to spare
She's a treasure, for I could travel the world
and no one like her exists anywhere
